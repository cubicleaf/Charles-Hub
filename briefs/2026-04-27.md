## 1. Anthropic locks Mythos 5 behind critical-infrastructure access wall

**Anthropic · April 7, 2026 · 20 days ago · Still developing**

Anthropic launched Claude Mythos Preview on April 7 as a restricted model available only to roughly 50 critical-infrastructure organizations under Project Glasswing—including AWS, Apple, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorgan Chase, Microsoft, and Nvidia—with $100M in usage credits. On Anthropic's own red-team findings, the model fully autonomously identified and exploited a 17-year-old RCE vulnerability in FreeBSD (CVE-2026-4747) and has found thousands of high-severity bugs across every major OS and browser. It is not publicly available; Anthropic says it won't release Mythos-class models generally until new safeguards are in place.

*Why it matters:* Anthropic is the first lab to formally publish a model it simultaneously considers too dangerous to release—a clear signal that the frontier is reaching capability thresholds that require access controls rather than just system prompts.

## 2. SpaceX public S-1 targets $1.75T Nasdaq valuation in June

**SpaceX · April 23, 2026 · 4 days ago · Still developing**

SpaceX's public S-1 targets a $1.75T valuation and ~$75B raise, which would make it the largest IPO in history. The roadshow is confirmed for the week of June 8, with a retail investor event June 11 and a likely listing window of June 18–30. SpaceX's $1.25T combined valuation came after its February acquisition of xAI; the company is allocating 30% of IPO shares to retail investors, three times the Wall Street norm. The strategic driver behind the xAI merger is orbital data centers running on 24/7 solar power.

*Why it matters:* A $1.75T listing reshapes index weights and institutional allocations across both space and tech sectors—and marks the first time Musk's AI and space infrastructure ambitions will be priced as a single public entity.

## 4. Stanford AI Index: talent inflow collapsed 80%, transparency scores declining

**Stanford HAI · April 13, 2026 · 14 days ago · Still developing**

The 2026 Stanford AI Index (400+ pages, released April 13) documents a field racing past its own guardrails. Key data points: AI researchers moving to the U.S. fell 89% since 2017, with an 80% drop in the last year alone; the Foundation Model Transparency Index average dropped to 40 from 58; on SWE-bench Verified, AI scores climbed from 60% to nearly 100% in one year; and on OSWorld (agentic computer tasks), accuracy hit 66.3%—within 6 points of human performance. Organizational AI adoption reached 88%.

*Why it matters:* The talent number is the most alarming data point: the U.S. is losing the researcher pipeline at the exact moment AI capability is compressing toward human-level performance across multiple domains.

## 5. DeepSeek V4 on Huawei silicon establishes export-control workaround at scale

**DeepSeek · April 24, 2026 · 3 days ago · Still developing**

DeepSeek V4-Pro (1.6T total parameters, 49B active, MIT license) scores 80.6% on SWE-bench Verified and is priced at $1.74/M input tokens—roughly 60% below comparable Western APIs. The release pairs with Huawei Supernode technology, combining Ascend 950 chip clusters to run V4 at frontier scale entirely on domestic Chinese hardware. V4-Flash (284B/13B active) targets cost-sensitive workloads at even lower price points.

*Why it matters:* This is the first demonstrated deployment of a frontier-class open-weight model on non-U.S. silicon at production scale. It establishes that chip export controls are no longer a reliable ceiling on Chinese AI capability.
