## 1. Blue Origin's New Glenn Destroys Its Own Launchpad
**Blue Origin · May 28, 2026 · 2 days ago**

New Glenn's engine system triggered an anomaly during a static fire test at Launch Complex 36, Cape Canaveral, igniting a fire that destroyed the rocket and significantly damaged Blue Origin's only launch complex at the site. The test was in preparation for a June 4 launch. The explosion follows an April 19 orbital failure — New Glenn's third flight — that left the second stage unable to reach a safe orbit and opened an active FAA investigation. No injuries were reported.

*Why it matters:* New Glenn is one of NASA's certified heavy-lift vehicles for Artemis payloads; with LC-36 now out of service for an unknown repair window and an ongoing FAA probe, the Artemis crew-landing schedule faces a new critical-path constraint with no clear recovery date.

## 2. Wix Cuts 1,000 Jobs and Names AI as the Cause
**Wix · May 28, 2026 · 2 days ago**

CEO Avishai Abrahami announced a 20% workforce reduction — roughly 1,000 of 5,277 employees — citing AI productivity gains making entire job categories redundant alongside currency headwinds from a strong shekel. The company grew revenue 14% year-over-year to $541M in Q1 2026 but posted a $57.5M net loss as operating expenses surged from 21% to 35% of revenue. Wix simultaneously disclosed its $80M acquisition of AI app-builder Base44, now at $150M ARR.

*Why it matters:* Unlike most companies that soften AI-driven reductions with restructuring euphemisms, Wix named the cause directly — setting a candor precedent that will intensify regulatory and public scrutiny of AI displacement across the SaaS sector.

## 3. DOJ Charges Google Engineer With Polymarket Insider Trading
**DOJ / Google · May 27, 2026 · 3 days ago**

Federal prosecutors charged Michele Spagnuolo, a Google software engineer residing in Switzerland, with money laundering, commodities fraud, and wire fraud. Under the alias "AlphaRaccoon," Spagnuolo allegedly accessed Google's unpublished 2025 "Year in Search" data and placed $2.75M across 25 Polymarket contracts on which search terms would trend — netting $1.2M in profit. He was arrested Wednesday in New York.

*Why it matters:* This is the second federal Polymarket criminal case in five weeks — the first involved an Army Special Forces sergeant betting on classified Venezuela intelligence — confirming that the DOJ views prediction-market manipulation as a securities-law-equivalent offense and will pursue tech insiders who exploit proprietary data to trade it.

## 4. Musk Contradicts SpaceX's Own IPO Filing on Anthropic Compute Deal
**SpaceX / Anthropic · May 28, 2026 · 2 days ago**

SpaceX's S-1 IPO filing describes Anthropic's access to all 220,000+ GPUs at the Colossus 1 Memphis data center — valued at roughly $1.25B/month — as a three-year agreement running through May 2029, with 90-day mutual termination. Elon Musk publicly contradicted this: "This is a 180 day lease with 90 day notice mutual cancellation thereafter. The short term was our request, not Anthropic's." The original deal was announced May 6; Musk's rebuttal landed May 28. Anthropic has not publicly resolved the discrepancy.

*Why it matters:* A CEO's posts directly contradicting his company's securities filing on a material contract raises disclosure questions ahead of SpaceX's IPO — and signals that even nine-figure-per-month AI compute deals may carry far less committed duration than the headline numbers imply.

## 5. Chinese Labs Reach Frontier-Class Coding AI at Sharply Lower Cost
**DeepSeek, Kimi, MiniMax, Z.ai · April–May 2026 · Still developing**

Four Chinese labs released open-weights models in close succession: DeepSeek V4-Pro (1.6T params, MIT license, Huawei Ascend 950 clusters), Moonshot Kimi K2.6, MiniMax M2.7, and Z.ai's GLM-5.1. All four benchmark at or near the Western frontier on agentic coding and engineering tasks. DeepSeek V4-Pro's API output costs $3.48/M tokens; comparable Western frontier models run $15+/M. All support self-hosting.

*Why it matters:* Open weights plus sub-$4/M inference pricing compresses Western AI providers' pricing power and accelerates a dynamic where marginal cost of frontier-class inference converges toward zero — changing the economics for every enterprise evaluating build-vs-buy for AI infrastructure.

## Under the radar
### NVIDIA Ising: AI Models for Quantum Error Correction
**NVIDIA · April 14, 2026 · 46 days ago**

NVIDIA released Ising, a family of open-source AI models built specifically for quantum computing infrastructure. The flagship is a 35B-parameter vision-language model that automates qubit calibration (cutting setup time from days to hours) and achieves 2.5× faster, 3× more accurate quantum error-correction decoding. Adopters already include Fermilab, Harvard SEAS, IQM Quantum Computers, and the UK National Physical Laboratory.

*Why it matters:* QEC decoding is one of the two hardest bottlenecks blocking fault-tolerant quantum compute; if AI can systematically accelerate it, the timeline for quantum advantage on practical problems moves meaningfully closer.
