## 1. Anthropic Glasswing expands: Claude Mythos found 10,000+ critical zero-days
**Anthropic · June 2, 2026 · 9 days ago** · Still developing

Anthropic expanded Project Glasswing from a small pilot to roughly 200 organizations across 15+ countries, giving critical infrastructure operators — power, water, healthcare, communications, hardware — access to Claude Mythos Preview, an unreleased frontier model a full capability tier above Opus 4.7. Partners collectively reported over 10,000 high- and critical-severity flaws in every major OS and browser in under two months. The initiative has since expanded again: Anthropic shipped Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5, making a safeguarded Mythos-class model generally available for the first time.

*Why it matters:* AI can now find and exploit software vulnerabilities at a scale and speed that fundamentally reframes offense/defense economics for every organization running critical infrastructure software.

## 2. Broadcom, Apollo, Blackstone launch $35B AI infrastructure platform
**Broadcom / Apollo / Blackstone · June 9, 2026 · 2 days ago**

Apollo led a $35 billion initial financing tranche for the Broadcom AI XPV Platform, co-anchored by Blackstone Credit & Insurance. The platform is structured to deploy 20+ gigawatts of compute capacity using Broadcom's custom XPUs and networking for Anthropic and OpenAI through 2028. The first phase — over one gigawatt — starts deploying at Fluidstack-operated sites in mid-2026.

*Why it matters:* Capital markets are now treating frontier AI compute like toll-road or energy infrastructure — attracting the same utility-grade financing structures that historically fund power grids and pipelines.

## 3. Microsoft Build 2026: MAI-Thinking-1 and Majorana 2
**Microsoft · June 2, 2026 · 9 days ago** · Still developing

At Build 2026 in San Francisco, Microsoft launched seven in-house models. MAI-Thinking-1 is a 35B-active-parameter sparse Mixture-of-Experts reasoning model (~1T total parameters, 256k context) trained from scratch on commercially licensed data with no distillation from OpenAI or any third-party model. It scores 97.0% on AIME 2025 and 94.5% on AIME 2026, and is preferred over Claude Sonnet 4.6 in blind side-by-side evaluations. Microsoft also unveiled Majorana 2: a redesigned topological qubit chip that replaces aluminum with lead in the superconducting material stack, reportedly achieving 20-second qubit lifetimes — more than 1,000x longer than predecessors — with a 2029 commercial-scale target. Outside physicists remain skeptical; the work is not yet peer-reviewed.

*Why it matters:* MAI-Thinking-1 marks Microsoft's first serious move toward model independence from OpenAI; Majorana 2, if the stability claims survive peer review, would put fault-tolerant quantum computing on a credible five-year path.

## 4. DOE finalizes U.S. fusion energy roadmap; Focused Energy closes $240M Series A
**DOE / Focused Energy · June 9–10, 2026 · 1–2 days ago**

The Department of Energy published its finalized Fusion Science and Technology Roadmap on June 9, establishing the first unified national strategy for commercializing fusion by the 2030s — spanning science, infrastructure, workforce development, and regulatory coordination with the NRC. The same week, Focused Energy, a laser-inertial confinement fusion startup, closed an oversubscribed $240 million Series A, bringing its total private capital raised to $300 million.

*Why it matters:* For the first time, federal strategy and private capital are moving on synchronized timelines for fusion, reducing the credibility gap that has plagued prior commercialization cycles.

## 5. Google ships Gemini 3.1 Ultra and Gemini 3.5 Live Translate
**Google · Early June 2026** · Still developing

Gemini 3.1 Ultra introduces a 2-million-token context window working natively across text, image, audio, and video without transcription intermediaries; Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite delivers 2.5x faster responses and 45% faster output vs. prior versions. Separately, Gemini 3.5 Live Translate performs real-time voice-to-voice translation while preserving the original speaker's tone, pacing, and pitch.

*Why it matters:* The 2M-token native multimodal context and identity-preserving voice translation set a new baseline that competing labs will need to match to stay relevant in enterprise and consumer deployment.
