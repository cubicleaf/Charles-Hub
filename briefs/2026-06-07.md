## 1. SpaceX Roadshow Opens for the Largest IPO in History
**SpaceX · June 4, 2026 · 3 days ago** · Still developing

SpaceX launched its investor roadshow on June 4 targeting a fixed $135-per-share price, a $1.75 trillion valuation, and a $75 billion raise — more than 2.5× the previous record set by Saudi Aramco's 2019 listing. *Why it matters:* Starlink's satellite network, NASA launch contracts, and xAI's compute infrastructure all become publicly auditable for the first time, forcing disclosure about assets that have operated with no external financial scrutiny.

## 2. Anthropic Files Confidential S-1 After $965B Series H
**Anthropic · June 1, 2026 · 6 days ago** · Still developing

Anthropic filed a confidential S-1 following a $65B Series H led by Altimeter, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, and Sequoia — the round pushed its post-money valuation past OpenAI's for the first time, to $965B. The company reported a $47B annualized revenue run-rate and projected $10.9B in Q2 2026 revenue, more than doubling Q1's $4.8B. An October listing window is targeted.

*Why it matters:* An Anthropic IPO would be the first pure-play frontier AI lab to go public, giving markets a hard price signal for closed-model AI companies and directly pressuring OpenAI's own IPO timeline.

## 3. Bipartisan Federal AI Act Would Preempt State Laws for Three Years
**Congress · June 4, 2026 · 3 days ago**

Reps. Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA) released a 269-page discussion draft of the Great American Artificial Intelligence Act of 2026. The bill imposes safety, transparency, and catastrophic-risk management requirements on AI developers with over $500M in annual revenue — covering OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, and xAI — and would preempt state-level AI development regulations for three years, with a sunset clause.

*Why it matters:* The three-year preemption clause would override California's AI safety law and dozens of pending state bills, concentrating all regulatory authority federally at the exact moment states have been the only meaningful source of AI oversight.

## 4. Nvidia RTX Spark: First Arm+Blackwell Superchip for Windows PCs
**Nvidia · June 2, 2026 · 5 days ago**

Announced at Computex 2026, the RTX Spark combines 20 Arm CPU cores (co-designed with MediaTek) with a Blackwell GPU carrying 6,144 CUDA cores, 128GB of LPDDR5X unified memory, and up to 300 GB/s memory bandwidth — delivering 1 petaflop of AI compute. It is the first Windows laptop chip to run the full CUDA software stack natively. Asus, Dell, HP, Lenovo, Microsoft, and MSI ship RTX Spark devices in fall 2026.

*Why it matters:* Native CUDA on Arm ends the x86 dependency that has kept Windows AI workloads cloud-bound or discrete-GPU-bound, and makes local inference of 120B-parameter models with 1M-token context a consumer product.

## 5. OpenAI Ships Dreaming V3 — ChatGPT's Biggest Memory Overhaul
**OpenAI · June 4–5, 2026 · 2–3 days ago**

OpenAI began rolling out Dreaming V3, a hierarchical background memory system that continuously synthesizes context across all ChatGPT conversations rather than waiting for explicit "remember this" prompts. Internal evals show factual recall rising from 67.9% (2025 system) to 82.8%, with serving compute dropping 5×. Rollout is live now for Plus and Pro users; Free tier and enterprise customers follow in coming weeks.

*Why it matters:* Persistent, high-accuracy memory across sessions is the primary blocker for AI displacing repeat-interaction software like CRMs, personal assistants, and coding environments — closing this gap is a direct assault on those product categories.
