*Two once-in-a-generation IPOs, a trillion-dollar AI lab entering the public queue, and Apple’s most consequential product refresh in years—all landing in the same week.*

## 1. Apple WWDC 2026: AI-rebooted Siri debuts as Tim Cook delivers his final keynote
**Apple · June 9, 2026 · Today**

Apple opened WWDC 2026 with a full relaunch of Apple Intelligence, centered on a rebuilt Siri that can reason across apps, manage multi-step tasks, and run core inference on-device without cloud routing. The conference is Tim Cook’s last as CEO before he steps down later in 2026. Apple is framing the Siri reboot as a direct correction to last year’s Intelligence rollout, which shipped fragmented and behind schedule and handed the early-mover narrative to Google and OpenAI.

*Why it matters:* An on-device agent at iPhone scale would change the privacy-versus-capability tradeoff that every AI competitor has accepted as fixed—and hand Apple a moat no cloud-dependent rival can replicate.

## 2. SpaceX locks $135 IPO price; Nasdaq listing three days out
**SpaceX · June 3, 2026 · 6 days ago** · Still developing

SpaceX set a fixed offering price of $135 per share on June 3, targeting 555.6 million Class A shares for a $75 billion raise and a $1.77 trillion total valuation—which would rank it seventh-largest in the U.S. by market cap, above Tesla. The Nasdaq debut under ticker SPCX is scheduled for June 12. Q1 2026 revenue came in at $4.7 billion with an operating loss of $1.9 billion; Starlink carries margins. A new sentence added to the amended S-1/A drew attention from Fortune as a possible signal of a Tesla-SpaceX combination, which neither company has confirmed.

*Why it matters:* At $1.77T, this would be the largest IPO in U.S. history; how public markets price it will set the valuation benchmark for Anthropic and OpenAI listings expected to follow shortly after.

## 3. Anthropic files confidential S-1 at $965 billion valuation
**Anthropic · June 1, 2026 · 8 days ago** · Still developing

Anthropic submitted a draft Form S-1 to the SEC on June 1, making it the first major AI lab to formally begin the public offering process ahead of OpenAI. The filing follows a $65 billion Series H that pushed the private valuation to $965 billion. Annualized revenue has crossed $47 billion, driven by enterprise adoption of Claude in coding and agentic pipelines. Share count and price are not yet set; timing depends on SEC review and market conditions.

*Why it matters:* Anthropic going public before OpenAI resets the competitive narrative and gives markets their first priced benchmark for large-scale AI lab unit economics.

## 4. Microsoft launches 7 MAI models at Build—none trained on OpenAI data
**Microsoft · June 2, 2026 · 7 days ago** · Still developing

At Build 2026 in San Francisco, Microsoft unveiled seven in-house models under the MAI brand from its AI Superintelligence Team: MAI-Thinking-1 for reasoning, MAI-Code-1 for code generation, plus five additional models covering voice, images, and documents. All were trained on clean, licensed data with no distillation from OpenAI—a pointed departure from the partnership’s history. MAI-Code-1-Flash is already live in VS Code via GitHub Copilot’s model picker and is available on Fireworks AI, Baseten, and OpenRouter. Microsoft describes it as cost-competitive with Anthropic’s Haiku class.

*Why it matters:* Microsoft building a competitive in-house model stack reduces OpenAI’s leverage in the partnership and signals that cloud incumbents are closing the capability gap with frontier labs on their own terms.

## 5. Majorana 2 claims 1,000× qubit stability gain; physicists push back hard
**Microsoft · June 2, 2026 · 7 days ago**

Microsoft unveiled Majorana 2, replacing aluminum superconductors with lead in its topological qubit stack. The company says this extends qubit lifetime from microseconds to a mean of 20 seconds—occasionally exceeding one minute—and has moved its commercial-scale quantum target to 2029, half the original timeline. Materials iteration was credited in part to agentic AI through Microsoft Discovery. However, Scientific American and Science News report that prominent physicists say the topological qubit mechanism has never been independently verified; Microsoft retracted a high-profile Nature paper on the same approach in 2021.

*Why it matters:* If the lead-stack improvement survives outside review, it is a genuine inflection point for fault-tolerant quantum computing; if it doesn’t, it puts Microsoft’s quantum credibility at serious risk ahead of its commercial timeline.

## Under the radar

**NVIDIA sweeps Korea AI infrastructure deals · June 6–8, 2026 · 1–3 days ago**

NVIDIA finalized strategic partnerships with six South Korean conglomerates—SK Hynix, SK Telecom, Naver, Hyundai, LG, and Doosan—spanning AI memory chips (SK Hynix is NVIDIA’s primary HBM4 supplier), cloud infrastructure, robotics, and autonomous mobility. The breadth suggests NVIDIA is locking multi-industry vertical control in one of the world’s most concentrated tech ecosystems ahead of intensifying competition from AMD and domestic Korean alternatives.

*Why it matters:* HBM supply agreements with SK Hynix double as competitive moats—if NVIDIA controls allocation, rivals running the same GPU architectures face a memory bottleneck.

**Gemini 3.5 Flash ships at frontier speeds · June 2026**

Google confirmed Gemini 3.5 Flash is generally available at 284 tokens/second with an Intelligence Index score of 55, priced at $1.50 input / $9 output per million tokens. The release directly competes with GPT-5.5 Instant and Anthropic’s Haiku tier.

*Why it matters:* Cheap, fast frontier-class inference commoditizes the low end of the API market—compressing margins for startups built on reselling AI access without differentiated value on top.
