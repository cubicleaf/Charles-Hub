## 1. China Forces Meta to Unwind Its $2B Manus Acquisition
**Meta / China NDRC · April 27, 2026 · 4 days ago**

China's National Development and Reform Commission ordered Meta to reverse its completed $2 billion acquisition of Manus, a Singapore-based AI startup with Chinese roots, after a months-long probe that was elevated to Xi Jinping's National Security Commission — the first time that body intervened in a cross-border tech M&A case. Manus employees had already joined Meta's AI team and investor payouts had cleared before the block arrived; Bloomberg reported April 29 that the Manus model is now "officially dead." Meta acquired the company in December 2025 after Manus had relocated its offices from Beijing to Singapore.

*Why it matters:* China has now demonstrated it can unwind completed foreign acquisitions of AI assets, adding a new and retroactive enforcement tool to an already fractured global AI investment landscape.

## 2. Huawei's Ascend 950PR Pulls ByteDance's $5.6B; Nvidia's China Share at Risk
**Huawei / ByteDance · Ongoing · Still developing**

Huawei's Ascend 950PR — rated at 1.56 petaflops FP4 and 2.8× the performance of Nvidia's H20 — entered mass production with 750,000 units planned for 2026. ByteDance committed $5.6 billion in orders; Alibaba followed. The chip ships with a CUDA-compatible software stack, lowering migration friction for labs currently on Nvidia tooling. Bernstein analysts estimate Huawei's share of China's AI chip market could climb to 50%, while Nvidia's may fall to 8% under current export restrictions. Huawei projects $12 billion in AI chip revenue for 2026, up 60% from $7.5B in 2025.

*Why it matters:* CUDA compatibility removes the biggest software-side barrier for Chinese AI labs switching off Nvidia, accelerating hardware decoupling faster than export controls alone had anticipated.

## 3. Meta Launches Muse Spark — Its First Proprietary Frontier Model
**Meta Superintelligence Labs · April 8, 2026 · 23 days ago · Still developing**

Meta released Muse Spark on April 8, the debut model from Meta Superintelligence Labs — the unit Zuckerberg stood up after concluding Llama was not keeping pace with OpenAI and Anthropic. Muse Spark scores 52 on the Intelligence Index, excels at visual STEM reasoning, health queries, and multimodal tasks, and is rolling out to WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Messenger, and Meta's AI glasses. Unlike Llama, it ships with no open weights; Meta said it hopes to open-source future versions. CNBC reported April 28 that Wall Street is still pressing Zuckerberg for a monetization roadmap.

*Why it matters:* Meta's pivot to a closed proprietary model is the clearest signal yet that open-source positioning alone was not delivering competitive frontier performance.

## 4. OpenAI Targeting Q4 IPO With $25B Annualized Revenue and $1T Valuation
**OpenAI · Ongoing · Still developing**

OpenAI crossed $25 billion in annualized revenue as of February 2026 — up from $6 billion at end-2024, a roughly 4× jump in 14 months. The company is targeting a Q4 2026 IPO priced toward a $1 trillion valuation, with a reported confidential SEC filing in Q2 and a listing window in October or November. OpenAI has told prospective investors it aims for $280 billion in annual revenue by 2030. The company is projected to lose approximately $14 billion in 2026 on compute and infrastructure costs despite the revenue surge.

*Why it matters:* If it prices near $1 trillion, OpenAI's listing immediately resets how public markets value every AI company below it.

## 5. Photon Teleported 270 Meters Through Open Air Between Two Quantum Dots
**Sapienza University of Rome · April 29, 2026 · 2 days ago**

Researchers at Sapienza University of Rome achieved the first successful teleportation of a photon's polarization state between two physically separate quantum dots linked by a 270-meter free-space optical channel. The system used GPS-assisted timing, ultra-fast single-photon detectors, and atmospheric stabilization to reach a state fidelity of 82 ± 1% — exceeding the classical limit by more than 10 standard deviations. Results were published in *Nature Communications*.

*Why it matters:* Relaying quantum states between independent emitters over open air is a foundational requirement for metro-scale quantum networks that don't depend on fiber infrastructure.

## Under the radar

**Imec Launches SPINS: €50M European Spin Qubit Pilot Line · April 2026**

Imec kicked off SPINS, a 25-partner European consortium with €50 million in funding, to industrialize spin qubits on 300mm semiconductor wafer infrastructure — the same process nodes used for classical chips. Fraunhofer IPMS, CEA-Leti, and Infineon are among the partners. *Why it matters:* Getting qubits onto 300mm fabs is the clearest path to manufacturing quantum processors at semiconductor scale; this is the most coordinated European push to do so yet.

**Anthropic MCP Crosses 97 Million Installs · March 2026 · Still developing**

Anthropic's Model Context Protocol reached 97 million installs as of March, up from near zero at its broad launch. MCP is now the dominant integration layer for AI agents connecting to external tools and data sources. *Why it matters:* A protocol crossing 97M installs in roughly a year is transitioning from proposed standard to de facto infrastructure, giving Anthropic significant platform leverage even as it competes at the model layer.
