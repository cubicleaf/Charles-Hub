## 1. Microsoft Build 2026: MAI-Thinking-1, Copilot Super App, Windows Local AI
**Microsoft · June 2, 2026 · Today**

Satya Nadella opened Build 2026 in San Francisco declaring "Agents are the new operating system for work," with three concrete deliverables: MAI-Thinking-1, Microsoft's first in-house dedicated reasoning model unveiled by AI chief Mustafa Suleyman; a reworked GitHub Copilot that operates as an autonomous coding agent running parallel specialized sub-agents (linting, testing, docs, security review) inside VS Code; and Windows Local AI, which runs AI agents entirely on-device via NPUs in Snapdragon X Elite, Intel Lunar Lake, and AMD XDNA hardware. Office 365 Copilot Agent Mode — persistent agents embedded directly in Word, Excel, Teams, and Outlook — rolls out to M365 subscribers in late June. GitHub already switched to usage-based AI Credits billing on June 1.

*Why it matters:* Whoever owns the default IDE agent owns developer mindshare at a pivotal moment — Microsoft is moving before Apple's WWDC and before Google ships Gemini 3.5 Pro, betting that distribution depth beats raw benchmark performance.

## 2. AMD EPYC Venice — Industry's First 2nm HPC CPU Enters Volume Production
**AMD · May 2026 · ~2 weeks ago**

AMD's sixth-generation EPYC Venice has become the first HPC chip to enter volume production on TSMC's N2 (2nm) node, starting at TSMC's Taiwan fabs with Arizona to follow. Venice ships with up to 256 cores, 1.6 TB/s memory bandwidth, and a 1.7× performance-per-watt uplift over the prior-gen Turin chips on 3nm. AMD CEO Lisa Su simultaneously committed $10B to Taiwan's AI packaging ecosystem. The Helios rack-scale platform — pairing Venice with Instinct MI450X GPUs — targets multi-gigawatt data center deployments in H2 2026.

*Why it matters:* Agentic AI inference shifts the workload mix such that data centers are moving from a roughly 1:4 CPU-to-GPU ratio toward 1:1; AMD just took the node lead over Intel on the silicon that now matters most.

## 3. Gemini 3.5 Pro — Google's June Launch Window Is Open
**Google · announced at Google I/O May 19, 2026 · 14 days ago** · Still developing

At I/O on May 19, Sundar Pichai told the audience "give us until next month" — publicly committing to a June GA date with no specific day. Gemini 3.5 Flash shipped that day and already beats Gemini 3.1 Pro on Terminal-Bench 2.1 (76.2% vs 70.3%) and MCP Atlas (83.6% vs 78.2%) at a cost of $1.50/M input, $9.00/M output. Pro is expected to arrive with a 2M-token context window — double Flash's and the largest of any production frontier model — and will target enterprise agentic workloads where Flash's cost tradeoff is insufficient. Prediction markets are pricing a late-June release above 80%.

*Why it matters:* A 2M-token context large enough to hold entire codebases or multi-year financial filings in a single prompt would materially change how enterprises architect AI pipelines — provided it ships before Microsoft's Build momentum closes the window.

## 4. Colorado AI Act Gutted — Effective Date Pushed to January 2027
**Colorado Governor Polis · May 14, 2026 · 19 days ago**

Governor Polis signed SB 26-189, stripping out the core of Colorado's original AI Act — the first comprehensive state AI regulation in the US. The original June 30, 2026 effective date is now January 1, 2027. More consequentially, the law's algorithmic-discrimination duty-of-care framework was eliminated outright: no more developer obligations to conduct risk impact assessments, maintain risk management programs, or file reports with the state AG. What survives is a narrow disclosure requirement around "automated decision-making technology" with a $20,000 per-violation civil penalty.

*Why it matters:* Colorado was the test case for US state AI regulation; its hollowing-out signals that the EU's high-risk-AI obligations going live in August 2026 are likely to be the only near-term binding compliance framework for multinational AI developers.

## 5. Anthropic in Talks for Second $30B Round at $900B Valuation
**Anthropic / Bloomberg · May 12–22, 2026 · 11–21 days ago** · Still developing

Anthropic is in advanced talks to raise at least $30B at a pre-money valuation above $900B — its second $30B raise in 2026, following a February Series G at $380B post-money led by GIC and Coatue. Sequoia, Dragoneer, Altimeter, and Greenoaks are each expected to commit roughly $2B. Bloomberg reported May 22 that the round could close within days. At a $900B pre-money figure, Anthropic would surpass SpaceX as the highest-valued private company on record.

*Why it matters:* Two $30B oversubscribed rounds in one year tests whether frontier model access can sustain toll-road economics at scale — and concentrates so much capital in two labs (Anthropic and OpenAI) that the barrier to entry for credible competition has effectively become a national-infrastructure-level investment.

## Under the radar

**3D Silicon Stacking Breakthrough — University of Illinois**
**U of Illinois Grainger College of Engineering · May 30, 2026 · 3 days ago**

Researchers demonstrated a method for building multiple layers of silicon transistors directly on top of one another without the thermal damage that has blocked monolithic 3D approaches. Unlike TSMC SoIC or Intel Foveros, which bond separately manufactured dies, the Illinois technique constructs transistors in layers on a single substrate. The work remains at lab scale, but the path to density gains independent of photolithography node advancement is the significant claim.

*Why it matters:* If the technique scales to fabrication, it opens a competitive dimension entirely separate from the 2nm → 1nm node race — potentially extending usable transistor density for years without requiring EUV lithography at shorter wavelengths.
