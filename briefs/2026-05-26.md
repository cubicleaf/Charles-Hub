## 1. Pope Leo XIV releases *Magnifica Humanitas* — AI's most consequential moral document yet
**Vatican · May 25, 2026 · 1 day ago**

Pope Leo XIV released his 42,300-word encyclical *Magnifica Humanitas* yesterday, framing AI as "the new industrial revolution" and calling for binding international regulation, a ban on autonomous lethal AI systems, and protection of workers from automation-driven "forced inactivity." The document singles out the concentration of AI power among "a small number of companies" and calls on states to subject AI firms to "stricter regulation." Anthropic co-founder Christopher Olah attended the Vatican launch. Pope Leo signed the document on May 15, the 135th anniversary of *Rerum Novarum*, the Church's landmark labor encyclical.

*Why it matters:* An encyclical carries moral weight with 1.4 billion Catholics and typically seeds decades of policy debate — this is the first time a papal document has addressed AI governance directly, and it will amplify calls for regulation in the EU, Latin America, and parts of the Global South.

## 2. Nvidia posts $81.6B revenue, guides Q2 at $91B — data-center growth 92% year-over-year
**Nvidia · May 21, 2026 · 5 days ago**

Nvidia reported Q1 FY2027 revenue of $81.6 billion — up 85% year-over-year and 20% quarter-over-quarter — with data-center revenue of $75.2 billion driven by the Blackwell 300 GPU ramp, InfiniBand, Spectrum-X Ethernet, and NVLink demand. Q2 guidance came in at $91 billion, above the high end of analyst estimates. Nvidia also announced an $80 billion stock buyback program and raised its quarterly dividend 25-fold to $0.25 per share.

*Why it matters:* Nvidia's quarterly numbers are the most reliable real-time gauge of global AI infrastructure capex — a $91B Q2 guide means hyperscaler buildout is still accelerating, not plateauing.

## 3. Google I/O 2026: Gemini Omni ships, AI Mode crosses 1B monthly users, Search overhauled
**Google · May 19, 2026 · 7 days ago · Still developing**

Google shipped Gemini Omni Flash — a multimodal model generating video, images, text, and audio from any input — across the Gemini app, Flow, and YouTube Shorts, with API access rolling out to developers. Gemini 3.5 Flash became the new global default in Search, where AI Mode now logs over 1 billion monthly users, with query volume more than doubling every quarter. Google also launched Gemini Spark, an always-on personal agent operating across Workspace and third-party apps, and Universal Cart, an AI-native shopping interface.

*Why it matters:* Google made explicit what has been building for 18 months — AI is the surface, not a feature on the highest-traffic website on the internet, and the 1B monthly user figure suggests the transition has crossed a threshold that is hard to reverse.

## 4. SpaceX Starship V3 completes Flight 12: first upgraded vehicle reaches Indian Ocean
**SpaceX · May 22, 2026 · 4 days ago**

SpaceX launched Starship V3 on its Flight 12 debut after a 24-hour scrub, completing most mission objectives before a controlled splashdown in the Indian Ocean. The Ship vehicle broke apart as planned on contact. V3 carries structural changes and upgraded Raptor 3 engines targeting the payload mass needed for Starlink V3 satellite batches and the NASA Artemis Human Landing System contract. SpaceX's Starlink constellation now exceeds 10,000 satellites.

*Why it matters:* V3 is the vehicle SpaceX's commercial and government revenue roadmap depends on — a clean Flight 12 moves the program meaningfully closer to operational status and keeps the NASA lunar timeline intact.

## 5. Anthropic's Mythos surfaces 1,596 zero-days in open-source code through Project Glasswing
**Anthropic · April 7 – May 22, 2026 · Still developing**

Claude Mythos Preview, Anthropic's restricted offensive-security model, has disclosed 1,596 vulnerabilities across 281 open-source projects since its April 7 launch through Project Glasswing, with 97 patches merged as of May 22. Mythos autonomously chains zero-day vulnerabilities into multi-step exploit sequences and is currently limited to a controlled consortium of 40+ organizations including AWS, Apple, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, CrowdStrike, and JPMorgan Chase. Anthropic is now preparing a narrower public channel through Claude Code and Claude Security for enterprise customers.

*Why it matters:* Mythos is the first frontier model Anthropic deemed too dangerous for general release — the controlled consortium rollout it pioneered may become the default governance model for the next capability tier.
