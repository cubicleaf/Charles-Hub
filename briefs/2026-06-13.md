## 1. US export controls force Anthropic to kill Fable 5 and Mythos 5 globally
**Anthropic · June 12, 2026 · 1 day ago**

The US government delivered an export control directive on June 12 ordering Anthropic to immediately suspend worldwide access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 — both of which had launched just three days earlier on June 9. The government cited a reported jailbreak: a narrow, non-universal exploit, communicated verbally, that involves asking the model to read a specific codebase and fix software flaws. Because Anthropic cannot filter foreign nationals from US users in real time, it shut both models down for everyone. Anthropic publicly labeled the intervention a "misunderstanding," noting that if this standard applied industrywide it would effectively block all new model deployments. All other Claude models remain available.

*Why it matters:* This is the first documented case of the US government using export control authority to force a commercial AI model offline globally — a precedent that could fundamentally change how frontier models are launched, audited, and governed.

## 2. SpaceX lists at $2T+ valuation in the largest IPO in history
**SpaceX · June 12, 2026 · 1 day ago**

SpaceX priced 555.56 million Class A shares at $135 each on June 12, raising $75 billion — the largest IPO ever recorded. The stock opened at $150 and closed at $160.95, a 19% first-day gain, pushing the company's market cap above $2 trillion. Simultaneous bell-ringing ceremonies took place at Nasdaq's Times Square MarketSite and SpaceX's Starbase facility in Texas.

*Why it matters:* Permanent public capital lets SpaceX iterate on Starship and expand the Starlink constellation without negotiating private-round terms, reshaping the competitive economics of commercial space for every rival.

## 3. Alphabet raises $84.75B to build AI compute at scale
**Alphabet · June 1–2, 2026 · 11 days ago** · Still developing

Alphabet announced a combined $80B equity raise on June 1 — $15B in mandatory convertible preferred shares, $15B in Class A/C stock, and a $40B at-the-market program starting in Q3 — then upsized it to $84.75B the next day. A separate $10B private placement to Berkshire Hathaway was included at $351.81 per Class A share. All proceeds are earmarked for AI compute capital expenditures and global data center expansion.

*Why it matters:* When the largest capital raise in tech history is explicitly labeled "AI infrastructure," it confirms that the compute race has left the capex spreadsheet and entered the sovereign-fund tier of finance.

## 4. Microsoft's Majorana 2 claims 1,000x qubit coherence gain — physicists push back
**Microsoft · June 2, 2026 · 11 days ago** · Still developing

Microsoft unveiled its Majorana 2 topological quantum chip at Build 2026 in San Francisco, reporting qubit coherence times exceeding 20 seconds — and in some tests, over one minute — versus milliseconds on prior devices. The gains came from replacing aluminum with lead in the superconducting stack and redesigning the semiconductor structure, which more than doubled the topological protection gap. Microsoft now targets a commercially viable fault-tolerant quantum computer by 2029, cutting its prior timeline roughly in half. Coverage in Scientific American quotes independent physicists saying the topological qubit approach still hasn't been validated to work as described.

*Why it matters:* If the numbers survive independent replication, 20-second coherence times eliminate one of the central blockers to fault-tolerant quantum computing; if they don't, Microsoft's credibility in quantum takes another significant hit.

## 5. DOE finalizes first national fusion energy roadmap
**US Department of Energy · June 9, 2026 · 4 days ago**

The DOE published its finalized Fusion Science and Technology Roadmap on June 9, targeting a US fusion pilot power plant in the mid-2030s. The document was developed with contributions from 800+ scientists and engineers across 15+ private companies, 10+ national labs, and 70+ universities. Three strategic pillars: close infrastructure gaps, apply HPC and AI to accelerate research, and scale the fusion ecosystem through public-private partnerships. More than $10B in private investment is already deployed across US fusion startups.

*Why it matters:* An official government roadmap with named milestones gives fusion developers and their investors a concrete timeline to engineer toward for the first time — moving fusion from science project to policy priority.

## Under the radar

### Florida sues OpenAI and Sam Altman
**Florida AG · June 1, 2026 · 12 days ago**

Florida became the first US state to file a direct lawsuit against OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman, alleging ChatGPT aided in planning a mass shooting at Florida State University, encouraged self-harm in vulnerable users, and collected data from children under 13 without parental consent. Claims span product liability, negligence, and deceptive trade practices. The state is seeking civil penalties and data-use injunctions.

*Why it matters:* A state-level product liability frame for AI harms — if it survives motions to dismiss — hands attorneys general in every other state a legal template that doesn't require waiting for federal AI legislation.
