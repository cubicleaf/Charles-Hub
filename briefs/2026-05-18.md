## 1. Anthropic's Mythos Forces Pentagon Policy Reversal
**Anthropic · April 7, 2026 · 41 days ago** · Still developing

Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview — withheld from public release — can autonomously identify zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser, and produced a working exploit for a 27-year-old OpenBSD flaw during red-team testing. The Pentagon responded by designating Anthropic a "supply chain risk" and blocking federal use of Claude. Multiple agencies defied the ban, citing Mythos' defensive value as essential. The White House has since re-engaged CEO Dario Amodei to negotiate a restricted-access framework modeled on Anthropic's Project Glasswing, which gives a small set of vetted partners early access to help defenders outpace attackers.

*Why it matters:* This is the first time an AI model's capabilities have directly triggered a U.S. federal agency designation and forced a live White House negotiation over access to private AI infrastructure.

## 2. Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI in Annual Revenue
**Anthropic · April 2026 · ~30 days ago** · Still developing

Anthropic's annualized recurring revenue reached $30 billion in April 2026, surpassing OpenAI's confirmed $24 billion ARR — the first time any competitor has topped OpenAI in that metric. The pace: $1B ARR in January 2025 to $30B in April 2026, a 30x move in 15 months. Over 1,000 enterprise customers now spend more than $1 million annually, doubling from 500 in February. OpenAI's CRO disputes the figure by roughly $8 billion, arguing Anthropic's methodology double-counts cloud-partner revenue flowing through AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.

*Why it matters:* Anthropic — still private, raising on venture terms — now generates more verifiable API revenue than OpenAI, shifting the assumption that OpenAI leads the commercial AI race.

## 3. SpaceXAI Division Forms; Cursor Buyout Option at $60B
**SpaceX · May 6, 2026 · 12 days ago** · Still developing

xAI was formally dissolved May 6, with Grok, the X platform, and the Colossus 1 supercluster in Memphis now operating under a new internal division called SpaceXAI. Excess Colossus capacity is being leased to Anthropic. Separately, SpaceX announced April 21 a deal giving it an option to acquire AI coding startup Cursor for $60 billion by year-end — or pay a $10 billion break fee for their ongoing collaboration — preempting a $2 billion fundraise Cursor had been preparing at a $50 billion valuation. Both moves are structured around SpaceX's anticipated summer IPO, where public stock would finance the Cursor purchase.

*Why it matters:* Combining xAI's model infrastructure, Grok's social data, Colossus' compute, and Cursor's developer tooling under a single IPO entity creates an AI-to-aerospace vertical with no direct public-market comparable.

## 4. Apple and Intel Sign Domestic Chip Manufacturing Deal
**Intel / Apple · May 2026 · ~1 week ago**

Apple and Intel reached an agreement for Intel to manufacture a portion of Apple's processors in the United States, confirmed by the Wall Street Journal. The deal — part of broader domestic supply-chain reshoring driven by CHIPS Act incentives — represents Intel's highest-profile manufacturing customer since its foundry push began. Intel stock has more than tripled in 2026; AMD is up roughly 25% in a single week after beating Q1 estimates, and is in talks with Samsung for 2nm production capacity. Nvidia's gains are comparatively modest at 15% YTD as Wall Street rotates toward other semiconductor plays.

*Why it matters:* Apple's shift toward domestic Intel manufacturing is the strongest market signal yet that the U.S. foundry ecosystem is functional, not aspirational — and that Intel's turnaround has cleared a credibility threshold.

## 5. China Trains Frontier-Grade 744B Model Entirely on Huawei Hardware
**Zhipu AI (Z.ai) · February 2026 · ~3 months ago** · Still developing

Zhipu AI's GLM-5 — 744 billion parameters — was trained on 100,000 Huawei Ascend 910B processors using the MindSpore framework, with zero NVIDIA hardware at any stage. On major benchmarks it performs within single digits of GPT-5.2 and Claude Opus 4.5. The model was released open-source under an MIT license. Inference runs on Ascend, Moore Threads, Cambricon, and Kunlunxin chips, making the full stack — training and deployment — domestically sourced.

*Why it matters:* GLM-5 is the first independently verified proof that NVIDIA export controls have not stopped China from reaching the frontier; the debate has shifted from "will controls work" to "did they work in time."

## Under the radar

**EU Parliament Delays High-Risk AI Act Deadlines to December 2027**
**European Parliament · May 2026 · ~1 week ago**

The European Parliament voted to push the high-risk AI system compliance deadline from 2025 to December 2, 2027, and extended watermarking requirements to November 2026. The package includes a new categorical ban on AI "nudifier" systems generating sexually explicit images of identifiable individuals. The delay reflects how far behind EU member state enforcement agencies remain in building review capacity.

*Why it matters:* A two-year slip in the EU's flagship AI law removes near-term regulatory pressure from frontier AI systems and cedes first-mover compliance leverage to whatever framework the U.S. assembles.

**OpenAI Acquires TBPN, Its First Media Property**
**OpenAI · May 2026 · ~2 weeks ago**

OpenAI acquired TBPN (Technology Business Programming Network), a Silicon Valley daily talk show on track to generate $30 million in revenue this year, for a price described as "low hundreds of millions." It's OpenAI's first media acquisition, part of a broader push to build a consumer superapp around ChatGPT and GPT-5.5.

*Why it matters:* An AI lab owning a tech media outlet raises fresh questions about editorial independence — and signals OpenAI is building content distribution infrastructure, not just API revenue.
