*Microsoft Build and Computex 2026 dropped back-to-back hardware bets this week — one on quantum supremacy, one on reinventing the PC — while the AI industry signaled its readiness for public markets.*

## 1. Microsoft Majorana 2: Qubit Stability Jumps 1,000x, Practical Machine Targeted for 2029
**Microsoft · June 2, 2026 · 1 day ago**

Unveiled at Build 2026 in San Francisco, Majorana 2 replaces aluminum with lead as its superconductor and adds indium arsenide antimonide to the semiconductor active region — a material stack Microsoft says improves qubit mean lifetime to 20 seconds (some qubits hit one minute), a 1,000x improvement over Majorana 1. The company used its Discovery agentic AI to automate chip fabrication measurements and analyze decades of research data during design. Microsoft has halved its original timeline and now targets a scalable, practical quantum computer by 2029.

*Why it matters:* If qubit stability holds up under independent review, this collapses the window between today's noisy intermediate-scale devices and the error-corrected machines needed to crack encryption and simulate molecular biology at scale.

## 2. Nvidia RTX Spark: Nvidia Enters the PC Silicon Market
**Nvidia / MediaTek · June 1–2, 2026 · 1–2 days ago**

Announced at Computex in Taipei and confirmed at Microsoft Build, the RTX Spark (N1X) is a consumer PC superchip co-developed with MediaTek delivering 1 petaflop of AI compute and 128 GB of unified memory alongside a custom CPU. Microsoft, Dell, HP, Asus, Lenovo, and MSI are building Windows PCs on it launching later this year. Intel and AMD shares fell sharply on the news; Nvidia gained more than 6%.

*Why it matters:* Nvidia extending GPU-era dominance into the CPU-integrated PC chip market is a structural threat to x86's 40-year hold on consumer silicon and puts capable local AI agent execution on commodity hardware.

## 3. Anthropic Files Confidential S-1 — Revenue Run Rate at $47B
**Anthropic · June 1, 2026 · 2 days ago**

Anthropic submitted a draft Form S-1 confidentially to the SEC less than a week after closing a $65 billion Series H that valued the company at $965 billion — above OpenAI's most recent $852B mark. The company reported a $47B annualized revenue run rate in May 2026, up from $10B in full-year 2025 revenue. Share count and price are unset; timing depends on SEC review and market conditions.

*Why it matters:* A near-$1T AI infrastructure IPO would reset valuations across the sector and force public-market investors to price foundation-model risk for the first time.

## 4. SpaceX IPO Roadshow Starts Thursday — $1.75T Target
**SpaceX · Ongoing · Still developing**

SpaceX filed its prospectus May 20, plans the roadshow starting June 4, prices the deal June 11, and lists on Nasdaq under SPCX on June 12, targeting a raise of up to $75 billion at a $1.75 trillion valuation — which would surpass Saudi Aramco's 2019 record of $35.4B raised. Full-year 2025 revenue was $18.7B (up 33% YoY); Starlink contributed $11.4B, or 61% of the total. Elon Musk retains 42% equity and 85% of votes under a dual-class structure.

*Why it matters:* The largest IPO in history would inject enormous institutional attention into space infrastructure and give Starlink a public-market benchmark for global satellite internet.

## 5. US Commerce Dept Commits $2B to Nine Quantum Companies
**Department of Commerce · June 2, 2026 · 1 day ago**

The Commerce Department issued letters of intent to invest $2 billion across nine quantum computing companies under the CHIPS and Science Act, with the government taking a minority equity stake in each. The announcement landed hours after Microsoft's Majorana 2 reveal, making June 2 the single largest day of quantum investment news on record.

*Why it matters:* Federal equity positions in quantum companies formalize the technology as strategic infrastructure — mirroring how CHIPS Act investments treated semiconductor fabs — and set a precedent for government co-investment alongside private capital.
