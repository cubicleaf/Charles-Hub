## 1. SpaceX Begins Trading as the Largest IPO in History
**SpaceX · June 12, 2026 · Today**

SpaceX set a fixed $135-per-share price—forgoing the usual bookbuild range—and begins trading on Nasdaq today as SPCX. The offering sells 555.6 million shares, raising $75 billion at a $1.77 trillion valuation, more than triple the size of Alibaba's 2014 record. Demand exceeded $250 billion; retail orders alone topped $100 billion, oversubscribing the deal roughly 4x.

*Why it matters:* SpaceX's debut puts the dominant orbital launch provider, Starlink broadband, and (following the February 2026 xAI merger) a significant AI compute stake into a single public market vehicle—a bundle with no historical precedent.

## 2. Apple WWDC: Gemini-Powered Siri Lands; Claude and ChatGPT Come to iPhone
**Apple · June 8, 2026 · 4 days ago · Still developing**

At WWDC 2026, Apple announced that the rebuilt Siri runs on Google Gemini under a ~$1B/year multi-year deal. iOS 27 introduces a three-tier AI Extensions system: on-device Apple Intelligence for private tasks, Gemini as the default cloud model, and user-selectable integrations including Claude and ChatGPT. Siri ships as a standalone app with full access to email, messages, photos, and calendar data.

*Why it matters:* Apple's 2.2 billion active devices becoming a native distribution layer for rival AI models inverts the competitive dynamic—Claude and ChatGPT no longer need Apple to promote them; Apple needs them to compete with Android's more open AI stack.

## 3. Anthropic Files Confidential S-1 at $965 Billion Valuation
**Anthropic · June 1–2, 2026 · 11 days ago · Still developing**

Four days after closing a $65 billion Series H, Anthropic submitted a confidential S-1 to the SEC, reporting $47 billion annualized revenue as of May and projecting it will cross $50 billion by end of June. The filing targets a public debut in fall 2026. At $965 billion, Anthropic would be the second-largest AI company to go public after SpaceX.

*Why it matters:* If Anthropic, OpenAI, and SpaceX all list in 2026, public markets will be forced to simultaneously price three companies with trillion-dollar pretensions—a stress test for AI valuation discipline that has no parallel.

## 4. Bezos' Prometheus Raises $12B to Build an "Artificial General Engineer"
**Prometheus AI · June 11, 2026 · 1 day ago**

Prometheus—Jeff Bezos' industrial AI startup, co-CEO'd with former Verily co-founder Vik Bajaj—raised $12 billion in a Series B backed by JPMorgan, BlackRock, Goldman Sachs, and DST Global, valuing the company at $41 billion. The 150-person firm operates from San Francisco, London, and Zurich and is building what it calls an "artificial general engineer": an AI that takes complex physical products from concept through manufacturing.

*Why it matters:* Unlike software-focused AI labs, Prometheus targets the full engineering stack of the physical world—if it works, it compresses product development cycles in aerospace, defense, and heavy industry by orders of magnitude.

## 5. NEURA Robotics Closes $1.4B Series C—Europe's Biggest Humanoid Round
**NEURA Robotics · June 10, 2026 · 2 days ago**

German humanoid robotics startup NEURA Robotics raised up to $1.4 billion in a Series C led by NVIDIA, Amazon, Qualcomm, Tether, Bosch, Schaeffler, and the European Investment Bank, valuing the company at roughly $7 billion. Capital targets serial production of multi-million robots by 2030 and expansion of "NEURA Gyms"—real-world training environments for cognitive robots. The company reports a deployment pipeline exceeding $1 billion.

*Why it matters:* This is the largest funding round ever for a full-stack robotics company; the same hyperscalers funding frontier AI models are now backing the hardware that embodies them.

## Under the radar

**EU Orders Meta to Open WhatsApp Business API to Rival AI Chatbots · European Commission · June 9, 2026 · 3 days ago**
The Commission issued its first interim antitrust measure in 17 years, ordering Meta to restore free WhatsApp Business API access to competitors—including OpenAI and Anthropic products—within five working days. Meta called it regulatory overreach and announced plans to challenge the ruling; non-compliance risks fines up to 10% of global revenue.

*Why it matters:* Regulators just established that locking AI competitors off a 3-billion-user messaging platform triggers emergency intervention—a precedent that lands squarely on Apple, Google, and Microsoft's legal desks.

**Atom Computing Achieves First Multi-Round Neutral-Atom Quantum Error Correction · June 3, 2026 · 9 days ago**
Atom Computing demonstrated sustained, multi-round quantum error correction using a toric code on its neutral-atom hardware—the first time this has been achieved outside superconducting architectures. Logical qubit error rates decreased as the system scaled, satisfying a key threshold condition for fault-tolerant quantum computing.

*Why it matters:* Neutral atoms are cheaper to scale than superconducting qubits; only two companies have demonstrated sustained multi-round QEC, and this milestone quietly advances the realistic timeline to utility-scale quantum machines.
