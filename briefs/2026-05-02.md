## 1. Microsoft and OpenAI gut their exclusive cloud deal
**Microsoft / OpenAI · April 27 · 5 days ago** · Still developing

Microsoft and OpenAI tore up the exclusivity terms of their 2019 partnership. OpenAI gains a nonexclusive Microsoft IP license through 2032 and continues paying a capped 20% revenue share to Microsoft through 2030. Amazon's $50B investment in OpenAI, announced in February, was the proximate trigger: GPT models are now slated to appear on Amazon Bedrock within weeks, and Google is actively evaluating an OpenAI partnership that was previously contractually blocked. Microsoft loses Azure's status as the sole distribution channel for OpenAI models.

*Why it matters:* Every enterprise cloud vendor's AI moat just got thinner — Azure loses its OpenAI edge, AWS gains a frontier model partner, and OpenAI gains distribution leverage that reshapes enterprise AI procurement decisions this year.

## 2. DeepSeek ships V4-Pro-Max: 1.6T params, 1M context, open weights
**DeepSeek · May 2 · Today**

DeepSeek released V4-Pro-Max today, a mixture-of-experts model with 1.6 trillion total parameters (49B active per forward pass), a 1-million-token context window, and dual thinking/non-thinking modes. A hybrid Compressed Sparse Attention architecture cuts KV cache usage 73% versus V3.2 at equivalent quality. Pricing is $0.145/M input tokens — roughly 7× cheaper than GPT-5.5 or Claude Opus 4.7 — and weights are fully open. The model leads open-source benchmarks in agentic coding, math, and STEM reasoning.

*Why it matters:* If benchmark parity holds under independent scrutiny, DeepSeek has again demonstrated that open-source models can undercut closed-model pricing while matching frontier capability, placing sustained pressure on Anthropic's, OpenAI's, and Google's API revenue lines.

## 3. Apple posts record March quarter: $111.2B revenue, +17% YoY
**Apple · April 30 · 2 days ago**

### Commonwealth Fusion SPARC magnet ring on pace for summer 2026 completion
**Commonwealth Fusion Systems · January 2026–present** · Still developing

CFS installed the first of 18 SPARC reactor magnets in January and expects to close the full ring by late summer, keeping a 2027 first-plasma date on schedule.
