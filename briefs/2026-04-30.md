## 1. Musk on the stand in OpenAI trial; Altman expected next week
**OpenAI / Musk v. Altman · April 29, 2026 · Yesterday** · Still developing

Day 3 of the Musk v. OpenAI jury trial in Oakland saw Musk defend his claim that he was "a fool" for funding the lab without ironclad nonprofit protections. *Why it matters:* The verdict—or any settlement—will set a precedent for how AI nonprofits can commercialize, affecting every foundation-model lab with a mixed-structure governance model.

## 2. DeepSeek V4 Pro: 1.6T parameters, Huawei chips, GPT-5-tier benchmarks
**DeepSeek · April 24, 2026 · 6 days ago** · Still developing

DeepSeek released V4 Flash and V4 Pro on April 24, then slashed prices on April 27 in what analysts are calling a deliberate price-war move. V4 Pro carries 1.6 trillion total parameters (49 billion active), a 1-million-token context window, and a Codeforces rating of 3,206—placing it 23rd among human competitors. The headline detail: V4 is DeepSeek's first model optimized for Huawei's Ascend chips, a direct test of China's ability to compete without Nvidia hardware. V4 Pro input is priced at $0.145/M tokens; output at $3.48/M.

*Why it matters:* If Ascend-optimized training proves economically viable at this quality level, it blunts the strategic effect of U.S. chip export controls on Chinese frontier AI development.

## 3. China freezes all new robotaxi permits after Baidu mass outage
**China MIIT / Baidu Apollo Go · April 29, 2026 · Yesterday**

A cloud-service anomaly in early April severed communication between Baidu's dispatch platform and more than 100 Apollo Go vehicles in Wuhan, stranding passengers and snarling traffic at dozens of intersections. Yesterday, regulators announced a nationwide freeze on new autonomous-vehicle permits pending investigation. Baidu's Wuhan service is offline indefinitely; the company had planned Apollo Go break-even by end-2026 via fleet expansion. Regulators are now mandating that vehicles must be able to complete a trip or return to a parking area independently if cloud connectivity drops.

*Why it matters:* This is China's first major AV regulatory pullback, and the offline-resilience requirement it spawns will ripple through every robotaxi operator's architecture globally.

## 4. NVIDIA releases Nemotron 3 Nano Omni: 9x throughput, open weights
**NVIDIA · April 28, 2026 · 2 days ago**

NVIDIA launched Nemotron 3 Nano Omni, a 30B-parameter open multimodal model with a hybrid Mamba-Transformer MoE backbone that activates only 3 billion parameters per forward pass—a 10:1 sparsity ratio. It bundles a Parakeet speech encoder and a C-RADIOv4-H vision encoder into a single model with a 256K context window, delivering 9x the throughput of comparable open omni models. Weights, datasets, and training recipes are all public; the model is also available as an NVIDIA NIM microservice across 25+ platforms.

*Why it matters:* A production-quality, open-weight omni model at this efficiency level removes the primary cost barrier for deploying multimodal agents at the edge or in compute-constrained environments.

## 5. EU launches €50M SPINS pilot line for semiconductor spin-qubit chips
**imec / EU Chips Act · April 2026 · This month**

The SPINS consortium—coordinated by imec with 25 partners including Fraunhofer, CEA-Leti, Infineon, and TU Delft—launched a €50M EU-funded pilot line for manufacturing semiconductor-based spin qubits. It operates across three hardware platforms: Si/SiGe, Ge/GeSi, and SOI. Funding flows through the EU Chips Joint Undertaking, with the explicit goal of industrializing spin-qubit fabrication beyond what individual research labs can sustain.

*Why it matters:* Spin qubits are among the leading paths to fault-tolerant quantum computing; a 25-partner manufacturing pilot line accelerates the transition from research prototypes to production-grade chips on a timeline relevant to the 2028–2030 window.
