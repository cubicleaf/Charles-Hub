## 1. Anthropic Hits $30B ARR, Passes OpenAI
**Anthropic · Early May 2026 · ~9 days ago** · Still developing

Anthropic's annualized revenue crossed $30 billion in April 2026, surpassing OpenAI's $24–25B ARR — the first time any competitor has led in AI revenue since ChatGPT launched. The jump from $9B to $30B took four months. Over 1,000 enterprise customers now spend more than $1M per year on Claude, doubling the February count. OpenAI disputes the figure, arguing it overstates recognized revenue by ~$8B due to accounting methodology differences. Roughly 80% of Anthropic's mix is enterprise API; OpenAI remains ~60% consumer subscriptions.

*Why it matters:* Enterprise API revenue compounds differently than consumer subscriptions — it grows with customer headcount and usage, not seat caps — which suggests Anthropic's lead could widen even if OpenAI's product pipeline stays ahead.

## 2. Apple Confirms Intel Foundry Deal for U.S. Processors
**Apple / Intel · May 5–8, 2026 · 4–7 days ago** · Still developing

Bloomberg reported talks; the Wall Street Journal then confirmed Apple and Intel have reached an agreement for Intel to manufacture some Apple processors for U.S.-sold devices. Intel shares surged 13% on the initial report and another 14% on confirmation. No chip generations, volumes, node names, or delivery timelines have been disclosed publicly.

*Why it matters:* Apple is the most demanding fabless customer alive; even a partial Apple tape-out on Intel 18A would validate the process and fundamentally change the competitive calculus against TSMC for every other potential customer.

## 3. Nvidia–Corning $3.2B Optical Infrastructure Deal
**Nvidia / Corning · May 6–7, 2026 · 5–6 days ago**

Nvidia will invest up to $3.2B in Corning via warrants tied to Corning shares, funding three new U.S. manufacturing facilities in North Carolina and Texas. Corning will expand U.S. optical connectivity capacity 10× and fiber production by more than 50%, adding 3,000+ jobs. The arrangement is a long-term supply agreement — not just an equity play — ensuring Nvidia's AI factory buildouts have domestic optical interconnects rather than depending on Asian supply chains.

*Why it matters:* As GPU supply normalizes, optical interconnects are the next datacenter-scale bottleneck; locking in domestic capacity now is a supply-chain hedge timed well before AI cluster demand peaks.

## 4. Four Chinese Labs Ship Frontier Coding Models in 12 Days
**DeepSeek / Kimi / MiniMax / Z.ai · April 24–May 7, 2026 · 5–18 days ago** · Still developing

DeepSeek V4 Pro (1.6T-parameter MoE, 49B active parameters, 1M context), Kimi K2.6, MiniMax M2.7, and GLM-5.1 all shipped within a 12-day window. V4 Pro scores 80.6% on SWE-bench Verified — 0.2 points below Claude Opus 4.6's 80.8% — and a Codeforces competitive-programming rating of 3,206, topping GPT-5.5's 3,168. Output pricing on V4 Pro: $3.48 per million tokens, roughly 7× cheaper than comparable Western frontier models.

*Why it matters:* Chinese labs have demonstrated they can sustain top-tier agentic coding capability at inference costs that will structurally pressure Western pricing; the 12-day coordination window suggests a deliberate industry-wide strategy, not coincidence.

## 5. Google Disrupts AI-Assisted Mass Exploitation Attempt
**Google Threat Intelligence Group · May 11, 2026 · Yesterday**

Google's Threat Intelligence Group reported that it likely disrupted an effort by a hacker group to use AI tools to mount a "mass exploitation event" — automated, large-scale targeting of software vulnerabilities. No victim count, specific CVE class, or named threat actor was disclosed in the public report. The incident is among the first publicly confirmed cases of an adversary operationalizing AI for wide-scale infrastructure attack orchestration.

*Why it matters:* Commoditized AI tools are compressing the time between vulnerability disclosure and mass exploitation, demanding a proportional shift in defender response timelines.

## Under the radar

**MatX closes $500M Series B for inference-optimized AI chips** · May 11, 2026 · Yesterday  
MatX, building custom neural-network accelerators for data center inference workloads, raised $500M in a Series B led by Jane Street Capital at a $2.3B valuation. Jane Street's lead (a firm known for rigorous quantitative analysis, not hype-driven bets) adds credibility to the thesis that general-purpose GPUs will lose the inference cost war.

*Why it matters:* If multiple well-funded ASICs erode Nvidia's inference margin, Nvidia's long-term moat narrows to training — a smaller and slower-growing market.

**OpenAI acquires TBPN media property** · Late April/Early May 2026  
OpenAI bought TBPN, a daily Silicon Valley talk show on track for $30M in annual revenue, for a reported "low hundreds of millions" — its first media acquisition. TBPN stays editorially independent under OpenAI's strategy organization.

*Why it matters:* An AI lab owning a tech news outlet creates a direct conflict-of-interest structure; watch whether TBPN's OpenAI coverage changes and whether regulators flag the acquisition.
