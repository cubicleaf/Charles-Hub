## 1. Anthropic Tops $965B Valuation With Opus 4.8 and $65B Raise
**Anthropic · June 2, 2026 · 4 days ago** · Still developing

Anthropic closed a $65B Series H — the largest AI funding round in history — at a $965B post-money valuation, making it more valuable than OpenAI for the first time. Claude Opus 4.8 launched alongside the raise, posting the biggest benchmark gains in agentic terminal coding and financial analysis, and cutting flawed-code pass-throughs by 4× versus Opus 4.7. *Why it matters:* A near-trillion-dollar valuation signals that institutional capital now treats frontier AI as critical infrastructure, not a bet on a product category.

## 2. OpenAI Reasoning Model Disproves Erdős's 80-Year-Old Geometry Conjecture
**OpenAI · May 20, 2026 · 17 days ago** · Still developing

A general-purpose OpenAI reasoning model — not purpose-built for math — independently disproved the planar unit distance problem, a conjecture Paul Erdős posed in 1946 about how many unit-distance pairs can exist in a flat arrangement of points. The model found an entirely new family of constructions that beats the square grid, with a proof externally verified by professional mathematicians; Fields Medalist Tim Gowers called it "a milestone in AI mathematics." OpenAI released the full proof text and an edited summary of the model's reasoning trace.

*Why it matters:* An AI generating novel proof strategies in unsolved research-level math without task-specific training changes the plausibility curve for autonomous scientific discovery.

## 3. Microsoft Debuts OpenAI-Independent AI Models at Build 2026
**Microsoft · June 2, 2026 · 4 days ago**

At Build 2026, Microsoft unveiled MAI-Thinking-1, its first in-house reasoning model trained entirely without OpenAI data, alongside MAI-Code-1-Flash, a 5B-parameter coding model now rolling into GitHub Copilot across all plan tiers. MAI-Code-1-Flash outperforms Claude Haiku 4.5 on all four tested coding benchmarks, including a 16-point lead on SWE-Bench Pro (51.2% vs. 35.2%), and adapts its reasoning budget dynamically — staying terse on simple completions, spending more compute on complex agentic tasks.

*Why it matters:* Microsoft serving 100M+ Copilot users from its own model stack — rather than reselling OpenAI — structurally reshapes the most commercially significant AI partnership in the industry.

## 4. Helion Raises $465M to Build Fusion Power Plant for Microsoft
**Helion · June 4, 2026 · 2 days ago**

Helion closed a $465M Series G led by Thrive Capital at a $15.5B post-money valuation, funding the completion of Orion, its first commercial fusion plant. Microsoft is the anchor power buyer, with Helion under contract to deliver grid electricity by 2028 — a deadline that now has nine figures of fresh capital tied to it. Orion would be the first fusion reactor anywhere to sell electricity commercially.

*Why it matters:* A binding 2028 delivery commitment backed by serious capital converts "fusion is always 30 years away" from a running joke into a testable claim.

## 5. Commerce Dept. Commits $2B in CHIPS Act Funds to Nine Quantum Firms
**US Department of Commerce · May 21, 2026 · 16 days ago** · Still developing

NIST issued letters of intent to nine companies: IBM receives $1B to establish a superconducting quantum-wafer foundry; GlobalFoundries gets $375M for a multi-architecture quantum foundry; D-Wave, Rigetti, Quantinuum, PsiQuantum, Atom Computing, and Infleqtion each receive up to $100M for R&D toward fault-tolerant, utility-scale systems. Commerce takes minority equity stakes in return. IBM and GlobalFoundries together account for 69% of the total.

*Why it matters:* Routing CHIPS Act money explicitly into quantum computing treats domestic qubit manufacturing as a national-security-level supply chain priority on par with classical fabs.

## Under the radar
**Pacific Fusion prototype · June 2, 2026 · 4 days ago**

Pacific Fusion's shipping-container-sized pulser prototype delivered 440 gigawatts in an 80-nanosecond burst — results strong enough to unlock the next tranche of its Series A, which now exceeds $1 billion total.
