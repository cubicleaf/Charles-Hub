## 1. Anthropic targets $900B valuation in $50B funding round
**Anthropic · May 9, 2026 · 1 day ago**

Anthropic is finalizing a $50B round anchored by a $40B top-up from Google and a $5B tranche from Amazon bundled with a $100B AWS-spend commitment, valuing the company near $900B — roughly triple its 2025 peak. The company also separately signed a $1.8B compute deal with Akamai to expand inference capacity to the edge. At $900B, Anthropic would exceed the market cap of most Fortune 50 companies.

*Why it matters:* Capital at this scale transforms Anthropic from a research lab into permanent AI infrastructure that governments and enterprises cannot afford to route around.

## 2. IonQ-SkyWater shareholders approve $1.8B merger
**IonQ / SkyWater Technology · May 8, 2026 · 2 days ago**

SkyWater stockholders voted to approve IonQ's acquisition — $15 cash plus $20 in IonQ stock per share — clearing the final major hurdle before an expected Q2–Q3 close. SkyWater's facilities in Minnesota, Florida, and Texas become Regional Quantum Production Hubs; the combined company targets functional tests of 200,000-qubit QPUs by 2028. IonQ also posted $64.7M in Q1 2026 revenue and raised its full-year guidance alongside the vote.

*Why it matters:* A domestic US quantum chip foundry under IonQ's control breaks the quantum sector's dependence on overseas fab capacity and hands the company a strategically defensible supply chain.

## 3. DeepSeek V4 and Huawei's Ascend 950PR form a self-contained Chinese AI stack
**DeepSeek / Huawei · April 24, 2026 · 16 days ago** · Still developing

DeepSeek V4-Pro ships 1.6 trillion parameters (49B activated, MoE), a 1M-token context window, and top-ranked open-source agentic coding performance — priced at $3.48 per million output tokens, 86% cheaper than the closest Western frontier model. Within days of launch, ByteDance, Tencent, and Alibaba placed new orders for Huawei's Ascend 950PR, which delivers roughly 2.87× the compute of Nvidia's H20 using in-house HBM; Huawei is targeting 750,000 units shipped in 2026.

*Why it matters:* China's AI ecosystem is rapidly closing both the model and hardware gap while operating entirely outside the CUDA supply chain that US export controls were designed to restrict.

## 4. Congress schedules May 13 markup on AI chip export bills
**US House Foreign Affairs Committee · May 2026 · Still developing**

The House Foreign Affairs Committee set a May 13 markup for two chip-focused bills: the MATCH Act, coordinating export controls with allied nations, and the Chip Security Act, targeting illicit chip smuggling to foreign adversaries. Huawei's 2026 AI chip revenue is already projected at $12B, up from $7.5B in 2025, evidence that existing controls have not kept pace with Chinese domestic production.

*Why it matters:* The May 13 markup is the next concrete policy inflection point in the US-China semiconductor rivalry — outcome will shape allied export-control coordination for years.

## 5. OpenAI reaches $25B ARR as $122B funding round closes
**OpenAI · May 2026 · Recent**

OpenAI hit $25B in annualized recurring revenue while closing a $122B funding round at an $852B post-money valuation, anchored by Amazon, Nvidia, SoftBank, and Microsoft. Novo Nordisk simultaneously announced a strategic partnership to integrate OpenAI across its entire global operation, spanning drug discovery to manufacturing.

*Why it matters:* OpenAI's revenue velocity validates the AI-as-infrastructure thesis and sets the monetization benchmark every competing lab will be measured against.
