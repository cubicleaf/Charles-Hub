## 1. China vetoes Meta's $2B Manus AI acquisition
**China NDRC · April 27, 2026 · 1 day ago**

China's National Development and Reform Commission ordered the cancellation of Meta's $2 billion acquisition of agentic AI startup Manus, citing tech-transfer concerns. Manus — a Singapore-incorporated company founded by Chinese nationals — launched in March 2025 and raised $75 million at a $500 million valuation from Benchmark before Meta's offer materialized. Beijing has simultaneously directed domestic AI firms including Moonshot AI, Stepfun, and ByteDance to reject US capital in new funding rounds unless explicitly cleared by regulators.

*Why it matters:* China is now treating its frontier AI startups as strategic assets subject to export control, mirroring the US approach to advanced chips — signaling that AI company ownership will be a geopolitical flashpoint alongside hardware and data.

## 2. DeepSeek V4 preview released
**DeepSeek · April 24, 2026 · 4 days ago** · Still developing

DeepSeek published a preview of V4 in two variants: V4-Pro (1.6T parameters, 49B activated) and V4-Flash (284B parameters, 13B activated), both supporting a 1-million-token context window and released under the MIT License. V4-Pro achieves a Codeforces rating of 3,206 — the highest competitive-programming score any model has posted, topping GPT-5.5's 3,168. At the inference level, V4-Pro uses just 27% of the single-token FLOPs of DeepSeek-V3.2. API pricing comes in at $1.74/M input and $3.48/M output for Pro, compared with GPT-5.5's doubled rates.

*Why it matters:* A fully open-weight model at this capability level, priced an order of magnitude below the US frontier, compresses the timeline for any organization running its own model stack rather than paying for API access.

## 3. GPT-5.5 launches
**OpenAI · April 23, 2026 · 5 days ago** · Still developing

OpenAI released GPT-5.5, a unified multimodal model that processes text, images, audio, and video in a single architecture — the first time OpenAI has converged all modalities into one model. It scored 60 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, leading all tracked models. OpenAI describes it as meaningfully better on scientific and technical workflows, including drug-discovery tasks. The trade-off: API pricing doubled versus GPT-5.4. The model is rolling out to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise users in ChatGPT now.

*Why it matters:* Doubling the API price while claiming token efficiency gains will pressure enterprise buyers to do the math carefully — and gives DeepSeek V4 a direct commercial opening.

## 4. Nvidia Ising: open AI models for quantum error correction
**Nvidia · April 16, 2026 · 12 days ago** · Still developing

Nvidia released Ising, described as the world's first family of open-source AI models specifically designed to help quantum processors achieve practical error correction. The models are 2.5x faster and 3x more accurate than conventional decoding approaches on tested benchmarks. Early adopters include Fermilab, Harvard SEAS, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory's Advanced Quantum Testbed, IQM, Infleqtion, and the UK National Physical Laboratory. Nvidia's position is that near-term quantum systems will be hybrid — GPU plus QPU — and Ising is engineered for that architecture.

*Why it matters:* Reliable error correction is the single largest engineering barrier between today's noisy NISQ devices and fault-tolerant quantum computers; Nvidia anchoring this layer with open models could define the integration standard.

## 5. General Fusion Analyst Day — fusion's first public company takes shape
**General Fusion · April 22 – April 29, 2026 · Ongoing**

General Fusion is hosting its Analyst Day tomorrow (April 29) as it advances toward becoming the first publicly traded pure-play fusion energy company via a $1 billion SPAC merger with Spring Valley Acquisition Corp. III (SVAC). The combined company will list on Nasdaq under the ticker GFUZ, targeting a mid-2026 close. The company's Magnetized Target Fusion approach avoids superconducting magnets and high-powered lasers, and it has secured $107.7 million in a committed PIPE from institutional investors alongside the deal.

*Why it matters:* Capital markets have never had a pure-play fusion vehicle; tomorrow's Analyst Day will set the public benchmark for how investors price fusion energy timelines and technology risk.

## Under the radar

**Blue Origin New Glenn third flight: stage recovered, payload misdelivered · April 20, 2026 · 8 days ago**
New Glenn's third launch successfully recovered its first stage for the first time on a re-flown booster, a meaningful reusability milestone for Blue Origin. However, an upper-stage propulsion anomaly left the direct-to-cellphone satellite payload stranded in the wrong orbit. The recovery validates the launch vehicle architecture; the payload outcome is a setback for the satellite operator.

*Why it matters:* Reusability unlocks Blue Origin's path to competitive launch pricing, but reliable upper-stage performance is equally critical for customer confidence.
