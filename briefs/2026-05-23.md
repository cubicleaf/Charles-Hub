## 1. OpenAI Reasoning Model Disproves 80-Year Erdős Geometry Conjecture
**OpenAI · May 20, 2026 · 3 days ago**

A general-purpose reasoning model — not a math-specialized system — disproved the unit distance conjecture in discrete geometry, first posed by Paul Erdős in 1946. Instead of classical geometry approaches, the model connected the problem to algebraic number theory, producing an infinite family of counterexamples with a polynomial improvement over the previously believed-optimal square-grid construction. External mathematicians verified the proof and co-authored an explanatory companion paper; the OpenAI blog notes the model was given no special scaffolding or math-specific training.

*Why it matters:* A general-purpose model making a peer-reviewed original mathematical contribution — not just assisting a human — marks a qualitative shift in AI's role as a research tool.

## 2. Google I/O 2026: Gemini 3.5 Flash Shipping Now, Spark Agent in Beta
**Google · May 19–22, 2026 · This week**

Google launched Gemini 3.5 Flash as generally available via the Gemini API and AI Studio, outperforming Gemini 3.1 Pro on Terminal-Bench 2.1 (76.2%), GDPval-AA (1,656 Elo), and MCP Atlas (83.6%) benchmarks at roughly one-third the cost of comparable flagship models. Gemini Spark, a 24/7 personal AI agent built on the Antigravity platform, is entering beta for US Google AI Ultra subscribers next week. Google also redesigned Search to handle long-form conversational queries alongside traditional short queries, and YouTube is adding "Ask YouTube" for video-linked answers.

*Why it matters:* Google is shipping faster than its prior I/O cycles — flagship-rival performance at Flash pricing and an agentic product in users' hands within days, not quarters.

## 3. Nvidia Q1 FY2027: Record $81.6B Revenue, Data Center Up 92% YoY
**Nvidia · May 20, 2026 · 3 days ago**

Nvidia reported Q1 FY2027 revenue of $81.6 billion — up 85% year-over-year and 20% sequentially — beating the $78.86B analyst consensus by $2.76B. Data center revenue hit $75.2 billion (up 92% YoY, 21% sequentially), driven by the Blackwell 300 product ramp and expansion of NVLink and Spectrum-X Ethernet. Hyperscale customers held at ~50% of data center revenue; the remainder came from AI cloud providers, sovereign customers, and enterprise. Q2 guidance: $89–93B.

*Why it matters:* At 92% annual data-center growth with guidance still accelerating, Nvidia's results are the most direct real-time gauge of global AI infrastructure investment — and they show no sign of plateauing.

## 4. Meta Cuts 8,000 Jobs While Reporting Record Revenue, Raises AI Capex to $145B
**Meta · May 20, 2026 · 3 days ago**

Meta began notifying approximately 8,000 employees across three waves starting Wednesday — roughly 10% of its ~80,000-person workforce. Concurrently, 7,000 additional roles are being moved to AI-focused teams and 6,000 previously planned hires are canceled. CEO Mark Zuckerberg wrote in an internal memo that "success isn't a given" in AI, citing fierce competition. The cuts come the same week Meta reported a record $56B quarterly revenue; 2026 capex guidance was raised by up to $10B to $145B. US workers receive 16 weeks of base severance plus two additional weeks per year of tenure.

*Why it matters:* Layoffs during record revenue while AI capex grows by $10B shows the structural trade-off in real time — AI investment is consuming headcount budgets even at companies under no financial pressure to cut.

## 5. AMD Ryzen AI Max 400 "Gorgon Halo": 192GB Memory, Runs 300B+ Parameter LLMs Locally
**AMD · This week · Ships Q3 2026**

AMD announced the Ryzen AI Max 400 "Gorgon Halo" APU — a Zen 5 / RDNA 3.5 / XDNA 2 design that scales unified memory to 192GB (160GB usable as VRAM), making it the first x86 client processor able to run models above 300B parameters without cloud dependency. CPU tops out at 16 cores / 5.2 GHz; GPU at 40 compute units / 3.0 GHz. This is a 50% memory increase over the Ryzen AI Max 300 "Strix Halo." ASUS, HP, and Lenovo will ship systems in Q3 2026.

*Why it matters:* 300B-parameter inference on a laptop-class chip changes the privacy, latency, and cost calculus for enterprise AI deployments — the cloud-only inference assumption breaks down at this memory tier.
