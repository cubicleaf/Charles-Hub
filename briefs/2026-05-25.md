## 1. SpaceX Starship V3 Clears Debut Despite Engine Anomaly
**SpaceX · May 22, 2026 · 3 days ago**

Starship's third-generation rocket — taller, heavier, powered by new Raptor 3 engines — launched from a newly built pad at Starbase, Texas on Flight 12. All 33 Super Heavy engines ignited cleanly; one of six upper-stage vacuum Raptors shut down mid-ascent, but the flight computer extended burns on the remaining five and maintained the planned trajectory. The vehicle deployed 20 mock Starlink satellites plus two live sensor satellites that scanned the heat shield during reentry, then splashed down in the Indian Ocean roughly an hour after liftoff.

*Why it matters:* V3 is the variant NASA has contracted to land astronauts on the moon under Artemis III; a clean debut clears the biggest technical gate before crewed missions.

## 2. SpaceX Files for History's Largest IPO · Still developing
**SpaceX · May 20, 2026 · 5 days ago · Still developing**

SpaceX released its public S-1 on May 20, confirming plans to list on Nasdaq under SPCX and raise $75 billion at a $1.75–2T valuation — roughly 60× what Saudi Aramco raised in 2019. The filing reveals Q1 2026 operating losses of $662M in launch and $2.5B in its AI division, offset by $1.2B profit from Starlink. Elon Musk retains 85% of voting power. Trading could begin as early as June 12.

*Why it matters:* The IPO would give SpaceX public-market capital to accelerate Starship production and Starlink expansion while setting a valuation benchmark for the entire commercial space industry.

## 3. Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI in Revenue · Still developing
**Anthropic · April–May 2026 · Still developing**

Anthropic reached $30B annualized recurring revenue in April 2026 — up from $1B in December 2024 — passing OpenAI's $24B ARR while reportedly spending 4× less on model training. Eighty percent of Anthropic's revenue comes from enterprise customers. OpenAI disputes the comparison, arguing Anthropic's gross-revenue accounting overstates the figure by ~$8B; on a net basis, Anthropic's comparable number is closer to $22B.

*Why it matters:* The revenue reversal, driven by Claude's enterprise penetration, challenges the assumption that OpenAI's head start and consumer reach translate into durable market leadership.

## 4. Google I/O 2026: Gemini 3.5 Flash Redraws the Price-Performance Map · Still developing
**Google · May 19, 2026 · 6 days ago · Still developing**

Google shipped Gemini 3.5 Flash — outperforming Gemini 3.1 Pro on key benchmarks at under half the cost of comparable frontier models and up to 4× faster output than rivals. Also announced: Gemini Omni (any-input-to-any-output generation), Gemini Spark (an agentic assistant wired into Calendar, Gmail, and Drive), and Google Search's largest overhaul in 25 years, replacing blue-link results with AI-generated answers and a cross-site "Universal Cart."

*Why it matters:* Gemini 3.5 Flash's cost-speed profile squeezes OpenAI and Anthropic on price just as agentic workloads are becoming the primary enterprise deployment pattern.

## 5. Pope Leo XIV Releases AI Encyclical *Magnifica Humanitas*
**Vatican · May 25, 2026 · Today**

Pope Leo XIV today presented *Magnifica Humanitas* ("Magnificent Humanity"), the Catholic Church's first encyclical dedicated to AI governance. Signed May 15 — exactly 135 years after *Rerum Novarum*, the Church's foundational labor-rights text — the document calls for protecting human dignity throughout AI development and draws explicit parallels between the Industrial Revolution's disruption of labor and generative AI's current trajectory. Anthropic co-founder Christopher Olah joined the papal presentation.

*Why it matters:* The Vatican's formal entry into AI ethics — invoking centuries of Catholic social teaching on labor and human dignity — adds a powerful institutional voice to global AI governance at a moment when regulatory frameworks remain fragmented.
