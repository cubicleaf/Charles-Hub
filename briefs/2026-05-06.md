## 1. Cerebras Files $26.6B IPO With OpenAI Entangled on Three Sides
**Cerebras · May 4, 2026 · 2 days ago**

Wafer-scale AI chip maker Cerebras launched its IPO roadshow Monday targeting $115–$125 per share, a $26.6B valuation at the top end, and a May 13 Nasdaq debut under the ticker CBRS. The company reported Q4 2025 revenue of $510M (up 76% year-over-year) and carries a $20B+ compute supply contract with OpenAI through 2028. That same OpenAI is simultaneously Cerebras's largest customer, a $1B creditor with warrant-to-equity conversion rights, and has Sam Altman among its angel investors.

*Why it matters:* Public markets must now price whether Cerebras is a hardware company or a captive OpenAI supplier — the answer reshapes valuation logic for the entire AI infrastructure sector.

## 2. Pentagon Signs Classified AI Deals With Eight Firms, Shuts Out Anthropic
**DoD · May 1, 2026 · 5 days ago**

The Department of Defense signed agreements granting SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, AWS, Oracle, and Reflection access to classified networks for AI deployment. Anthropic was explicitly excluded after refusing to remove safety guardrails restricting warfare applications, per sources cited by CNN. This marks the first confirmed instance of a major AI lab losing a government contract specifically over safety policy.

*Why it matters:* Safety constraints now carry a quantifiable government-contract cost, and the current administration has publicly priced what level of AI risk it tolerates in defense settings.

## 3. DeepSeek V4 Optimized for Huawei Ascend, Breaks From Nvidia CUDA · Still developing
**DeepSeek / Huawei · April 24, 2026 · 12 days ago · Still developing**

DeepSeek released V4 as the first frontier open-weights model co-developed natively on Huawei's Ascend 950 architecture and CANN software stack, bypassing Nvidia CUDA entirely. DeepSeek offered a 75% developer discount through May 5; the announcement immediately spiked Ascend 950 orders. Huawei now projects $12B in AI chip revenue for 2026 as ByteDance, Tencent, and Alibaba rush to secure supply.

*Why it matters:* A mature Chinese AI-hardware stack — open-weights frontier model plus domestic silicon at scale — removes the last structural dependency on U.S. export-controlled chips for inference.

## 4. AMD and Intel Jointly Publish x86 ACE Spec: 16x AI Gain Over AVX
**AMD / Intel / x86 EAG · Late April 2026 · ~1 week ago**

Arch-rivals AMD and Intel released the AI Compute Extensions (ACE) white paper through the x86 Ecosystem Advisory Group, standardizing outer-product matrix acceleration across the full x86 stack from laptops to data centers. The spec claims 16x compute density over equivalent AVX10 operations at the same vector width, with native support for INT8, FP8, MXFP8, and BF16. Initial software integration targets NumPy and SciPy; first silicon is 1–2 years out, with AMD's Zen 6 EPYC "Venice" the earliest candidate.

*Why it matters:* A single x86 AI instruction standard reduces fragmentation costs for framework developers and positions the billion-device x86 installed base to meaningfully close the inference gap with purpose-built accelerators.

## 5. Commonwealth Fusion Applies to Join Power Grid — First Fusion Company to Do So
**Commonwealth Fusion Systems · April 30, 2026 · 6 days ago**

Commonwealth Fusion Systems filed to interconnect with a major U.S. power grid operator, the first fusion energy company to take this step. Its SPARC demonstration tokamak in Massachusetts is 75% built and on track to first plasma in 2027. CFS has already committed the output of its planned 400 MW Fall Line Fusion Power Station in Virginia to Google and Eni as anchor buyers.

*Why it matters:* Filing for grid interconnection shifts fusion from long-horizon research bet to near-term infrastructure procurement decision for utilities and corporate power buyers.
