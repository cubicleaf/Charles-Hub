US Central Command announced on 18 June 2026 that it had lifted the naval blockade it had enforced on Iranian ports and coastal areas for over two months, citing the Memorandum of Understanding signed earlier that week by Presidents Donald Trump and Masoud Pezeshkian to end the US-Iran conflict.

"Today, US forces lifted the blockade on all maritime traffic entering and exiting Iranian ports and coastal areas, in accordance with the President's direction. American forces are not impeding the transit of vessels to or from Iranian ports. All US military blockade enforcement efforts have ceased," CENTCOM stated.

The command added that US naval vessels would remain in the area and that the blockade could be reimposed if Iran failed to honor its commitments: "Our great Naval Ships will remain in the general area to make sure that all aspects of the agreement are adhered to, obeyed and in full force and effect."

The blockade lasted over two months and involved at least nine attacks on civilian commercial vessels. The week before its lifting, a US missile strike killed three Indian mariners — an incident Trump, when meeting Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi at the G7 summit in France, expressed no regret about. Oil prices had already begun falling following the MoU's announcement.

The CENTCOM announcement marks the operational end of the US military campaign against Iran that began in late February 2026. The MoU's terms, as reported, include a 60-day ceasefire window, reopening of the Strait of Hormuz to commercial shipping, technical nuclear talks, and potential sanctions relief. Iran has consistently stated that the deal requires Israel to end its war in Lebanon and withdraw from occupied Lebanese territory — a demand Israel explicitly rejects.

The lifting of the blockade removes a significant source of US leverage over Iran. Whether Trump would reimpose it to pressure Israel — which continued its Lebanon offensive in apparent defiance of the MoU — was already an open question as CENTCOM made its announcement. Follow-up implementation talks between US and Iranian delegations, with Pakistan and Qatar as mediators, were scheduled in Switzerland.

For Israel, the end of the US military campaign against Iran and the reopening of Iranian oil revenues represents a fundamental shift in strategic conditions. The war had been jointly launched by the United States and Israel in late February; its termination, negotiated without Israeli participation, removes the US military umbrella that had partially absorbed Iranian retaliatory capacity and leaves Israel continuing its Lebanon campaign against a more financially stable Iran.
