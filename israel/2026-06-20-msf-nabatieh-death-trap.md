Médecins Sans Frontières warned on 20 June 2026 that the southern Lebanese city of Nabatieh had become a "death trap," as Israel launched a wave of strikes across southern Lebanon in the hours after a ceasefire with Hezbollah had been announced — killing at least 50 people and preventing rescue teams from reaching casualties.

MSF said it was responding to an influx of attack victims at its Nabatieh facility, including paramedics who had been wounded during rescue missions. "The ongoing situation in Nabatiyeh is devastating. What our teams are describing resembles a death trap. People are caught under heavy shelling, while rescue teams are unable to safely reach them," said Pierre Boulet-Desbareau, MSF's emergency coordinator in Lebanon. "Civilians and first responders must be protected, and unhindered access for rescue teams is urgently needed." Patients were arriving with "severe head injuries, heavy bleeding, shrapnel injuries and in need of amputations."

Lebanese health officials confirmed at least 50 people killed in the renewed attacks. Israel stated that the strikes were launched in response to a Hezbollah attack that killed four Israeli soldiers. Despite US President Donald Trump having announced on Friday afternoon that Israel and Hezbollah had agreed to a ceasefire, Israeli forces continued launching attacks through Saturday. Lebanon's National News Agency reported that Israeli aircraft and drones carried out overnight strikes across Nabatieh, destroying residential buildings and homes.

The attacks directly disrupted planned follow-up implementation talks between US and Iranian delegations in Switzerland. Tehran reportedly delayed sending its negotiating delegation due to the escalated Israeli assault. The strikes on Nabatieh were among the events that led Iran to reclose the Strait of Hormuz on 20 June, citing Israel's continued offensive as a breach of the US-Iran MoU.
