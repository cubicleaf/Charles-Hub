Vice President JD Vance delivered an unusually sharp public rebuke of senior Israeli cabinet ministers on 18 June 2026 at a White House briefing, warning them that the United States was Israel's last remaining ally and that a country of nine million people could not "kill your way out" of its security problems.

Vance's comments targeted Israeli Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich and National Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir, both of whom had publicly rejected the US-Iran Memorandum of Understanding that President Trump had signed the day before with Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian.

"Donald J. Trump is the only head of state in the entire world who is sympathetic to the nation of Israel at this moment in time, and he happens to be the head of state of the world's superpower," Vance said at the briefing. "If I was in the cabinet of the Israeli government, I might not be attacking the only powerful ally that I have anywhere left in the entire world."

Vance pointed to US military support as the basis for his warning: "Over the last three months, two-thirds of the defensive weapons that have protected your homeland have been built by American hands and paid for by American tax dollars." He directly challenged the ministers' rejection of the MoU: "What is your exact proposal? You're a country of nine million people. You can't just kill your way out of solving every single national security problem that you have."

Despite the severity of Vance's language, there was no sign of any concrete consequence being threatened. Antiwar.com noted that the Trump administration had shown no willingness to leverage military aid to Israel or threaten to cut it off to compel an end to the Lebanon war. Iran, meanwhile, continued to condition the MoU's implementation on a full Israeli withdrawal from Lebanese territory. Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi stated: "The end of the war includes the end of occupation. Without the withdrawal of Israeli forces from territories they occupied during this war, the war will have not been fully brought to an end."

Israel's offensive in Lebanon continued regardless. Vance's remarks — the most direct public criticism of an Israeli government by a senior US official in recent memory — thus exposed a structural gap: the administration had made clear what it wanted from Israel without deploying any instrument to obtain it.
