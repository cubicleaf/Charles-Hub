Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu reaffirmed on 18 June 2026 that Israel would not withdraw its forces from southern Lebanon, directly contradicting the terms of the Memorandum of Understanding signed two days earlier between the United States and Iran to end the active phase of their conflict.

"We will restore security and prosperity to northern towns," Netanyahu said. "That requires maintaining the security zone in southern Lebanon; it requires that we not leave there, as long as Israel's security needs require it."

The Israeli military simultaneously published a new map on X showing an expanded occupation zone: "The IDF is deployed in the Security Zone, ~10 km inside Lebanese territory, due to operational requirements. IDF soldiers will continue to remove threats and strengthen the defense of Israel's northern residents." An Israeli official told Reuters that Israel was conducting "stubborn negotiations" with the Trump administration over maintaining the occupation, with no indication that Trump was prepared to leverage military aid to compel a withdrawal. Israel Hayom reported that Netanyahu was bracing for pressure from Trump but that no US demand for the withdrawal of Israeli troops had been issued.

Iran had consistently framed the MoU's implementation as contingent on Israeli withdrawal from Lebanon. The Iranian military warned of a "harsh response" if Israeli strikes continued — and they had not stopped: at least three people were killed in Lebanon on Thursday. Two days after Netanyahu's statement, Iran reclosed the Strait of Hormuz, citing the continued Israeli offensive as a breach of the agreement.

Netanyahu's public reaffirmation positioned his government in direct defiance of the MoU's central terms without the Trump administration yet applying concrete pressure to force compliance, creating the gap that Iran exploited to reimpose its most significant economic leverage.
