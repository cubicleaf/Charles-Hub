Israeli Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich announced on 19 May 2026 that he had been informed the International Criminal Court had requested an arrest warrant for him, calling it "a declaration of war" and immediately threatening to forcibly displace the Bedouin community of Khan al-Ahmar as retaliation.

Smotrich disclosed the warrant request at a press conference but did not reveal who had informed him. The ICC's warrant proceedings are confidential. "And in the face of a declaration of war, we will fight back with a vengeance," he said. He announced he would sign an order to evacuate Khan al-Ahmar — a Palestinian Bedouin community of roughly 180 people in the occupied West Bank whose demolition Israeli courts have authorized but which successive governments had delayed under international pressure.

The ICC's accusations reportedly center on Smotrich's orders for forced displacement of Palestinians, his support for relocating Israeli settlers into occupied Palestinian territory, and his publicly stated position that it may be "justified and moral" to starve Palestinians in Gaza. If the warrant is approved by ICC judges, Smotrich would become the third Israeli official targeted by the court, after Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu and former Defense Minister Yoav Gallant, against whom warrants for crimes against humanity and war crimes were issued in November 2024.

The Trump administration responded to the November 2024 warrants by sanctioning the court, cutting its judges and prosecutors off from banks, credit card companies, and technology platforms including Amazon. The sanctions have not halted ICC proceedings; the court has continued to function.

Smotrich also attacked the Palestinian Authority in his statement, calling it "the terrorist organisation mistakenly called the Palestinian Authority," reflecting the Israeli government's anger at Palestinian diplomatic support for international legal proceedings against Israeli officials.

The UK and four other countries sanctioned both Smotrich and fellow far-right cabinet minister Itamar Ben-Gvir the previous year, accusing them of repeatedly inciting violence against Palestinians in the West Bank. Smotrich has publicly called for the permanent conquest of Gaza and the re-establishment of Jewish settlements there — positions Netanyahu has formally rejected but which characterize the far-right coalition partners whose seats are essential to Netanyahu's governing majority.

The ICC warrant request follows a pattern: following the November 2024 Netanyahu/Gallant warrants, the court's escalation to sitting ministers signals a broadening of accountability proceedings. Smotrich's immediate announcement of the Khan al-Ahmar evacuation underscores the Israeli government's established practice of escalating settlement and displacement activity in direct response to international legal pressure.
