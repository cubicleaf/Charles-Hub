An Israeli drone strike in Gaza City killed at least three Palestinians on 18 June 2026, as Gaza's Health Ministry reported that Israeli forces had killed 1,007 Palestinians since the US-brokered ceasefire deal was signed — a milestone that coincided with renewed ground operations aimed at expanding Israeli territorial control of the Strip.

According to the Palestinian news agency WAFA, the strike hit a civilian vehicle in the al-Rimal neighborhood of Gaza City. Palestinian medics told Reuters that Israeli forces operating in central Gaza killed at least one additional Palestinian later that day.

The Health Ministry's daily update stated that "since the so-called ceasefire deal was signed, the IDF has killed 1,007 Palestinians in Gaza and wounded 3,165." The ministry's overall death toll since the start of the war in October 2023 reached 73,018 — a figure that, according to several independent studies, is likely a significant undercount, with the real violent death toll potentially as high as 100,000.

The killing came as Gaza residents reported a visible increase in Israeli ground operations aimed at seizing additional territory, consistent with Prime Minister Netanyahu's publicly disclosed directive ordering the IDF to expand from 60 percent to 70 percent of Gaza. The resumption of deliberate territorial expansion — alongside continued airstrikes — places Israel in systematic violation of the October 2025 ceasefire deal whose framework it had nominally accepted.
