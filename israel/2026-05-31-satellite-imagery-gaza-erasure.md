Newly updated satellite imagery of southern Gaza released in late May 2026 documented the near-total erasure of the territory's civilian infrastructure, as Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu disclosed he had ordered the military to expand Israeli control from 60 percent to 70 percent of the Gaza Strip.

The Saudi neighbourhood in Tal as-Sultan, a 752-unit housing project, has been flattened. In Khan Younis, Hamad City — a Qatari-funded complex of 53 multi-story buildings with approximately 3,000 units that sheltered an estimated 15,000 people — is a ruined shell surrounded by displacement tents.

"Scenes of searching for food are cruel, and we are on the verge of a famine that could rear its head at any moment," said Ola Abu Moamer, a Palestinian journalist in Khan Younis. "Many families return with empty pots from soup kitchens, without securing any food."
